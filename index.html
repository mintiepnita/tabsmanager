(Tabs Manager, 2024)

is a digital tool designed to transform the common phenomenon of tab hoarding into an organized and purposeful citation system. Inspired by the universal experience of accumulating countless open tabs—each representing tasks, ideas, or interests—this tool bridges the gap between digital browsing and meaningful information management.

With Tabs Manager, users can generate printable archive documents of their saved tabs. Each document includes screenshots of the tab contents along with detailed reference information, ensuring that no task, curiosity, or idea is lost to the chaos of modern browsing. This feature allows users to preserve their digital findings as a physical or digital reference, transforming rapid online explorations into lasting, actionable resources.

Developed from the project in Web to Print class at California College of the Arts (CCA) for Fall 2024, Tabs Manager redefines how we interact with and preserve digital information, turning tabs into lasting resources for productivity and creativity.
